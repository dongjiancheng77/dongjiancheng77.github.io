I developed  **Behavior-Equivalent Token (BE-Token)** , a context-compression method that learns a single token to replace long system prompts while preserving their behavioral effect on downstream tasks. This significantly reduces  prompt overhead , improves  inference efficiency, and frees up more context budget for user inputs in LLM applications.
